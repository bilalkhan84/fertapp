Male fertility is one of the most under-talked-about topics in healthcare.

It accounts for roughly half of all fertility challenges — but when a man gets back his semen analysis, he's handed a clinical report full of numbers with no clear roadmap for what to do next. "Your morphology is 3%. Borderline." Okay. Now what?

I couldn't find a tool that actually answered that. So I built one.

Meet FertTrack:

🧬 Dashboard — a daily check-in for the 4 habits that matter most: supplements, sleep, heat exposure, and exercise. With a live 90-day countdown to your retest.

📊 My Results — enter your semen analysis and get a plain-language breakdown of every number against WHO reference values. No medical background required.

📅 90-Day Plan — a guided week-by-week program built around the sperm maturation cycle. The habits you build today will show up in your retest 13 weeks from now.

🏥 Care & Coverage — an Ontario-specific guide covering what OHIP covers, how to find a fertility specialist, and how to navigate the system.

Building FertTrack opened something up for me.

I'm an Engagement Lead. I don't write production code. But using Claude and Supabase together, I shipped something I would've left in a Notion doc a year ago.

A few things that really clicked:

1️⃣ Start with a problem you actually understand.
I knew exactly who this was for and what it needed to do. That clarity sharpened every prompt, every decision, every trade-off. Vague ideas produce vague software.

2️⃣ Design before you build.
I used Claude to concept screens and user flows before touching the codebase. It's much easier to critique a UI than a live app. Seeing something tangible first helped me avoid building the wrong experience entirely.

3️⃣ Ask "why" constantly.
The biggest unlock with Claude wasn't the code it generated — it was being able to ask "what are the trade-offs here?" at every decision point. It stopped feeling like using a tool and started feeling like pairing with a senior engineer.

4️⃣ AI makes adjacent skills accessible.
I still can't write production code from scratch. But I can now explore architectural decisions, understand what I'm building, and ship things I would've previously handed to an engineer and hoped for the best.
